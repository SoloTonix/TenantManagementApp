Great direction. If **landlords are one side of the platform**, the **tenant side must also have clear goals and value**, otherwise tenants won’t engage with the app.

For a scalable SaaS like your **RentFlow / Tenant Manager**, the tenant experience should feel similar to modern financial apps like Opay or PalmPay — simple, clear, and centered around **payments, records, and communication**. 📱

Below is a **product-level breakdown** of the **tenant user goals, objectives, and functionalities**.

---

# 1️⃣ Core Goal of the Tenant App

The tenant side of the platform should solve one main problem:

> **Make rent management easy, transparent, and organized for tenants.**

Many tenants currently manage rent with:

* Bank transfers
* WhatsApp messages
* Paper receipts
* Verbal agreements

Your app centralizes all that.

---

# 2️⃣ Primary Objectives for Tenant Users

### 1. Rent Transparency

Tenants should always know:

* Their **rent amount**
* **Next due date**
* **Payment history**
* **Outstanding balance**

This reduces disputes between tenant and landlord.

Example:

```
Rent Amount: ₦450,000
Next Due Date: Sept 1
Outstanding Balance: ₦0
```

---

### 2. Easy Rent Payment

Tenants should be able to:

* Pay rent directly inside the app
* See confirmation instantly
* Receive digital receipts

Payment gateways you could integrate later:

* Paystack
* Flutterwave

---

### 3. Payment Records

Tenants should never lose proof of payment.

The app should store:

```
All payments
Payment date
Amount
Receipt
Payment method
```

This is extremely valuable during disputes.

---

### 4. Communication with Landlord

The app should simplify landlord–tenant communication.

Tenants can:

* Notify landlord of payment
* Report issues
* Ask questions

Example:

```
Maintenance Request:
Kitchen sink leaking
```

---

### 5. Lease Information

Tenants should see:

```
Property name
Unit number
Rent amount
Lease start date
Lease expiration date
```

This prevents confusion.

---

# 3️⃣ Core Tenant Functionalities

## 1. Tenant Dashboard

The first screen tenants see.

Key information:

```
Next Rent Due
Outstanding Balance
Recent Payments
Lease Status
```

Example:

```
Next Rent Due: Aug 1
Amount: ₦350,000
Status: Pending
```

---

## 2. Pay Rent

A dedicated payment screen.

Features:

* Pay rent
* Choose payment method
* Confirm payment
* Generate receipt

Later upgrades:

* installment payments
* automatic payments

---

## 3. Payment History

Shows all payments.

Example list:

```
June Rent - ₦350,000 - Paid
May Rent - ₦350,000 - Paid
April Rent - ₦350,000 - Paid
```

Tenant can tap to view receipt.

---

## 4. Maintenance Requests

Tenants should be able to report property issues.

Example categories:

```
Electrical
Plumbing
Appliances
General repair
```

Workflow:

```
Tenant reports issue
↓
Landlord receives notification
↓
Issue tracked until resolved
```

---

## 5. Notifications & Reminders

Tenants receive alerts for:

```
Rent due soon
Rent overdue
Maintenance updates
Lease renewal reminders
```

Example:

```
Your rent is due in 3 days.
Amount: ₦350,000
```

---

## 6. Digital Receipts

After each payment:

Tenant gets:

```
Receipt ID
Date
Amount
Property
Landlord name
```

This builds **trust in the system**.

---

# 4️⃣ Tenant App Screen Structure

Your tenant app could look like this:

```
Tenant Dashboard
   │
   ├── Pay Rent
   ├── Payment History
   ├── Lease Details
   ├── Maintenance Requests
   └── Notifications
```

This keeps the tenant side **simple and focused**.

---

# 5️⃣ Tenant User Journey

Typical flow:

```
Tenant invited by landlord
↓
Tenant installs app
↓
Tenant logs in
↓
Sees rent details
↓
Receives reminder
↓
Pays rent
↓
Gets receipt
```

The experience should feel **smooth and reliable**.

---

# 6️⃣ Why the Tenant Side is Critical

Many property apps fail because they only focus on landlords.

But tenant engagement improves:

* payment compliance
* transparency
* communication
* record keeping

It also makes your SaaS **much more valuable**.

---

Tenants enable **auto-pay** monthly.

---

### Expense Sharing

Split rent automatically.

### Credit Scoring

Track tenant payment history to build **tenant reputation**.

---

### Marketplace

Tenants can find new rentals inside the platform.

The tenant experience should be:

* simple

* payment-focused

Just like fintech apps such as Opay.
